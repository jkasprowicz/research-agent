# Core Literature

## 1. Emergency medicine complexity and laboratory interpretation

### Key references used

- Cruz AT. *Indications and Interpretation of Common Laboratory Assays in the Emergency Department*. Pediatr Clin North Am. 2018;65(6):1191-1204. doi:10.1016/j.pcl.2018.07.005.
  - Useful for framing routine laboratory interpretation in emergency care.

- Newman-Toker DE, Peterson SM, Badihian S, et al. *Diagnostic Errors in the Emergency Department: A Systematic Review*. AHRQ; 2022.
  - Useful for contextualizing diagnostic complexity, heterogeneity, and uncertainty in emergency settings.

### Potential additional references

- Reviews on ED crowding, triage complexity, and diagnostic uncertainty in mixed adult-pediatric emergency populations.
- Studies specifically addressing how routine laboratory testing is used in undifferentiated emergency presentations.

## 2. Real-world laboratory data and data quality

### Key references used

- Ma C, Wang X, Wu J, Cheng X, Xia L, Xue F, Qiu L. *Real-world big-data studies in laboratory medicine: Current status, application, and future considerations*. Clin Biochem. 2020;84:21-30. doi:10.1016/j.clinbiochem.2020.06.014.
  - Supports the importance and challenges of laboratory big data in real-world settings.

- Weiskopf NG, Weng C. *Methods and dimensions of electronic health record data quality assessment: enabling reuse for clinical research*. J Am Med Inform Assoc. 2013;20(1):144-151.
  - Foundational reference for completeness, correctness, and related data-quality dimensions.

- Kahn MG, Callahan TJ, Barnard J, et al. *A Harmonized Data Quality Assessment Terminology and Framework for the Secondary Use of Electronic Health Record Data*. EGEMS (Wash DC). 2016;4(1):1244.
  - Useful for structured discussion of secondary-use data quality.

- Lewis AE, Weiskopf N, Abrams ZB, et al. *Electronic health record data quality assessment and tools: a systematic review*. J Am Med Inform Assoc. 2023;30(10):1730-1740. doi:10.1093/jamia/ocad120.
  - Strong contemporary synthesis of EHR data-quality assessment methods.

- Ban Al-Sahab B, Leviton A, Loddenkemper T, Paneth N, Zhang B. *Biases in Electronic Health Records Data for Generating Real-World Evidence: An Overview*. J Healthc Inform Res. 2024;8(1):121-139. doi:10.1007/s41666-023-00153-2.
  - Important for discussing selection bias, information bias, and documentation bias in EHR-based studies.

### Potential additional references

- Validation studies on laboratory data harmonization across institutions.
- Methodological papers on missingness handling in observational clinical data.

## 3. Computational phenotyping and unsupervised clinical clustering

### Key references used

- Becker F, Smilde AK, Acar E. *Unsupervised EHR-based phenotyping via matrix and tensor decompositions*. Wiley Interdiscip Rev Data Min Knowl Discov. 2023;13(4):e1494. doi:10.1002/widm.1494.
  - Central conceptual reference for computational phenotyping.

- Johnson AEW, Ghassemi MM, Nemati S, Niehaus KE, Clifton D, Clifford GD. *Machine Learning and Decision Support in Critical Care*. Proc IEEE. 2016;104(2):444-466.
  - Useful for broader context around machine learning, routine clinical data, and translational caution.

- Nemati S, Mohammadi B, Hooshanginezhad Z. *Clustering Based on Laboratory Data in Patients With Heart Failure Admitted to the Intensive Care Unit*. J Clin Lab Anal. 2024;38(21).
  - Relevant example of laboratory-based clustering in a defined clinical syndrome.

### Potential additional references

- Studies comparing K-means with latent class analysis or mixture models in clinical phenotyping.
- Validation-focused phenotyping papers with external cohorts and outcome association.

## 4. Latent biological phenotypes in acute illness

### Key references used

- Seymour CW, Kennedy JN, Wang S, et al. *Derivation, Validation, and Potential Treatment Implications of Novel Clinical Phenotypes for Sepsis*. JAMA. 2019;321(20):2003-2017. doi:10.1001/jama.2019.5791.
  - Core comparator for latent phenotypes in acute heterogeneous illness using routine data.

- Calfee CS, Delucchi K, Parsons PE, Thompson BT, Ware LB, Matthay MA. *Subphenotypes in acute respiratory distress syndrome: latent class analysis of data from two randomised controlled trials*. Lancet Respir Med. 2014;2(8):611-620. doi:10.1016/S2213-2600(14)70097-9.
  - Landmark study for inflammatory subphenotypes in critical illness.

- Kellum JA, Prowle JR. *Paradigms of acute kidney injury in the intensive care setting*. Nat Rev Nephrol. 2018;14(4):217-230.
  - Useful for clinically grounded discussion of renal dysfunction patterns in acute illness.

### Potential additional references

- Studies linking inflammatory-renal overlap to outcomes in emergency or critical care cohorts.
- Papers on hepatic injury phenotypes in undifferentiated acute care populations.

## 5. Visualization and dimensionality reduction

### Key references used

- McInnes L, Healy J, Saul N, Grossberger L. *UMAP: Uniform Manifold Approximation and Projection*. J Open Source Softw. 2018;3(29):861. doi:10.21105/joss.00861.
  - Supports the use of UMAP strictly as visualization, not as independent validation.

### Potential additional references

- Methodology papers comparing UMAP and t-SNE in biomedical representation learning.
