## Candidate-by-Candidate Assessment

## 1. BMC Medical Informatics and Decision Making

### Fit

**Very good.** This is the cleanest conceptual home for the current manuscript.

The journal publishes work on health information technologies, health data reuse, analytics, and decision-making in health care, and explicitly does not base editorial decisions on perceived impact alone if the study is scientifically sound.

Sources:

- Scope/home: [BMC Medical Informatics and Decision Making](https://bmcmedinformdecismak.biomedcentral.com/)
- Submission guidelines: [Preparing your manuscript](https://bmcmedinformdecismak.biomedcentral.com/submission-guidelines/preparing-your-manuscript)

### Likely reviewer expectations

- clear real-world data provenance
- transparent preprocessing and clustering rationale
- honest limitation handling
- supplementary robustness material
- data availability statement and reporting transparency

### Methodological compatibility

**Good.** The journal is compatible with observational, exploratory informatics work if the manuscript is careful about claims.

### Novelty expectations

**Moderate.** The paper does not need to be groundbreaking, but it does need to be methodologically clear and clinically interpretable.

### Acceptance likelihood

**Moderate to moderately good** if submitted with the current conservative framing.

### Likely weak points

- no outcomes
- no external validation
- selected cohort from `n_exames >= 6`
- encounter-level recurrence

### What the current manuscript would require

**Moderate revision**, not major additional analyses.

The current robustness package is probably enough for this journal if the supplement is organized well.

### Formatting / submission expectations

- BMC research articles generally use a **structured abstract** and the Springer Nature research-article format commonly caps the abstract at **350 words**
- figure titles: max **15 words**
- figure/table legends: max **300 words**
- large tables and datasets can go into **additional files**
- supplementary material is fully normal and expected when needed

### Current risk

- abstract length: **probably acceptable** at ~326 words
- main text length: **safe**
- figure/table count: **safe**

## Best Journal by Submission Strategy

## 1. Safest submission strategy

**Submit to BMC Medical Informatics and Decision Making first.**

Why:

- best methodological tolerance for exploratory real-world analytics
- current robustness package is probably enough
- good scope alignment
- supplementary material is welcome
- lower dependence on outcomes than emergency or translational journals

Preparation before submission:

- translate and polish into strong academic English
- make the supplement highly navigable
- explicitly position as exploratory phenotyping / health-data reuse

## 2. Highest-upside realistic strategy

**Submit to JMIR Medical Informatics first, with BMC Medical Informatics and Decision Making as the fallback.**

Why:

- higher upside than BMC MIDM
- still a real fit
- manuscript could plausibly survive editorial screening if the informatics framing is strong

Risk:

- may still be judged not translational enough
- reviewers may want stronger implementation or validation depth

**JBPML**

## Formatting and Submission Risk Summary

## Abstract structure expectations

- **BMC MIDM / BMC Emergency / likely INTJEM**: structured abstract, commonly BMC-style `Background / Methods / Results / Conclusions`, typically around **350 words**
- **JMIR Medical Informatics**: structured abstract, up to **450 words**
- **JALM**: structured abstract, **250 words**
- **einstein**: structured abstract `Objective / Methods / Results / Conclusion`, **250 words**
- **JBPML**: structured abstract in Portuguese and English, more formal IMRD-derived structure

## Word count risks

- current main text `~2945`: **safe** for all shortlisted journals here
- current abstract `~326`: **safe** for BMC and JMIR, **too long** for JALM and einstein, likely needs tightening for some Brazilian targets

## Figure/table limits

- current main manuscript with **1 figure + 1 table** is safe everywhere
- **einstein** explicitly allows **no more than 4 figures**
- **JALM** allows **6 total tables/figures**
- **JMIR** recommends no more than **5 tables** and **8 figures** in the main article
- **BMC journals** are flexible but want oversized material shifted to supplements

## Supplementary material expectations

- **BMC journals**: supplementary files are normal and strongly compatible with this manuscript
- **JMIR**: multimedia appendices are highly acceptable and strategically useful
- **JALM**: supplemental data allowed
- **JBPML**: less clearly supplement-driven in the instructions, so main scientific support should remain visible in the primary manuscript
- **einstein**: do not rely on supplement to rescue weak main-text clinical anchoring

## Final Recommendation

## Best overall target right now

**BMC Medical Informatics and Decision Making**

## Best fallback ladder

1. **JMIR Medical Informatics** if you want more upside and accept more risk

## What I would not do first

- I would **not** lead with an emergency medicine journal unless you plan to add stronger clinical anchoring or outcome analyses.
- I would **not** lead with a laboratory prestige journal like **JALM** unless you want a more difficult review process and are willing to strengthen the laboratory-practice argument.
- I would **not** use **einstein** as the first shot unless you want a Brazilian indexed journal with broad clinical branding and are prepared to compress the abstract and possibly still face demands for stronger clinical relevance.
