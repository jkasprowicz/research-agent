# Multimodal Hematology Search Strategy

## Strategic Aim

This search strategy is designed specifically to test the doctoral positioning:

**Multimodal clinical-laboratory intelligence for hematology**

The goal is not only to find hematology AI papers. It is to detect whether the literature already supports a meaningful research niche at the intersection of:

- peripheral blood smear morphology
- CBC and structured laboratory data
- analyzer outputs and laboratory workflow
- multimodal AI and data fusion
- reasoning-aware, knowledge-grounded, or LLM-enabled decision support
- robustness and interpretability for real-world laboratory use

## Strategic Hypothesis

The strongest doctoral opportunity is likely to sit in a gap between two partially developed literatures:

1. **image-centric hematology AI**
   Usually focused on leukocyte or blood-cell classification from smear images.

2. **tabular laboratory or CDS literature**
   Usually focused on structured data, prediction, or decision support without detailed morphology integration.

The search should test whether the combination of these two literatures is:

- already mature
- emerging but fragmented
- or still sufficiently underdeveloped to justify a PhD

## What This Search Is Trying to Detect

### Signal 1. True multimodal hematology studies

These are the highest-priority hits:

- smear image + CBC
- smear image + analyzer flags
- smear image + structured laboratory variables
- smear image + metadata + clinical decision support

### Signal 2. Near-miss studies

These are strategically important even if they are not fully multimodal:

- image-only hematology AI with strong clinical workflow discussion
- CBC or laboratory prediction studies with no image component
- hematology decision support systems without modern AI
- general multimodal medical AI with methods transferable to hematology

### Signal 3. Emerging reasoning-aware literature

These may signal a higher-upside doctoral layer:

- LLMs in laboratory medicine
- knowledge graphs for clinical reasoning
- retrieval-augmented clinical support
- agentic or multi-agent workflows for decision support

## Strategic Search Tiers

## Tier 1. Core hematology multimodal retrieval

Main goal:

- find studies that already combine morphology and laboratory data

Priority concepts:

- blood smear / peripheral blood smear
- CBC / hemogram / analyzer flags
- multimodal / data fusion
- AI / decision support

Interpretation:

- If results are sparse, this supports novelty.
- If results are numerous but fragmented, this supports a synthesis-and-integration thesis.
- If results are already mature, the thesis should move toward robustness, reasoning, or implementation gaps.

## Tier 2. Hematology laboratory intelligence retrieval

Main goal:

- capture computational hematology papers that are not yet multimodal but still define the field

Priority concepts:

- hematology AI
- blood smear AI
- laboratory medicine AI
- decision support
- analyzer-assisted review

Interpretation:

- This tier helps define the baseline field and identify how image-centric it still is.

## Tier 3. Exploratory reasoning retrieval

Main goal:

- detect whether reasoning-aware AI is already entering hematology/laboratory interpretation

Priority concepts:

- large language models
- foundation models
- knowledge graphs
- retrieval augmentation
- agentic AI
- clinical reasoning

Interpretation:

- Sparse but relevant retrieval would support a forward-looking doctoral contribution.
- Heavy generic retrieval with low hematology specificity would suggest careful scoping is needed.

## Recommended Order of Execution

1. Run broad searches in PubMed, Scopus, and Embase.
2. Run focused searches in the same three databases.
3. Run SciELO multilingual searches.
4. Run Web of Science for citation-sensitive and cross-disciplinary retrieval.
5. Run IEEE Xplore and ACM Digital Library for methods and reasoning-system capture.
6. Deduplicate.
7. Screen using the defined labels.
8. Map the literature into clusters.

## Expected Literature Clusters

After screening, papers will likely fall into these groups:

### Cluster A. Image-only hematology AI

- leukocyte classification
- smear morphology recognition
- cell segmentation/detection
- CNN/ViT/YOLO studies

### Cluster B. Structured laboratory prediction

- CBC-based disease prediction
- laboratory triage models
- analyzer-driven workflows

### Cluster C. Integrated or multimodal hematology AI

- image + CBC
- image + analyzer
- image + metadata
- case-level multimodal diagnosis support

### Cluster D. Decision support and reasoning systems

- CDS systems
- rule-based or hybrid decision support
- LLM or KG-enabled laboratory reasoning

### Cluster E. Robustness / implementation studies

- domain shift
- uncertainty
- calibration
- interpretability
- external validation

## Decision Rules for Doctoral Positioning

### If Cluster C is sparse

Conclusion:

- Strong novelty case for the proposed PhD direction.

Recommended framing:

- build the bridge between morphology-centric AI and structured laboratory intelligence

### If Cluster C exists but is methodologically weak

Conclusion:

- Strong opportunity for a better-designed doctoral project.

Recommended framing:

- robust, interpretable, clinically realistic multimodal hematology intelligence

### If Cluster D is sparse in hematology

Conclusion:

- reasoning-aware and knowledge-grounded systems may be a high-upside secondary layer

Recommended framing:

- keep reasoning as a later or optional component built on top of multimodal hematology

### If Clusters A and B are strong but disconnected

Conclusion:

- this is probably the most favorable scenario

Recommended framing:

- your PhD can integrate two mature but underconnected evidence streams

## Strategic Inclusion Priorities

The most important studies for the final doctoral argument are:

1. multimodal hematology papers, however few
2. blood smear AI papers that discuss real laboratory use
3. CBC/laboratory decision support papers in hematology
4. reasoning/LLM/KG papers with explicit clinical-laboratory grounding
5. robustness and external validation papers in hematology AI

## Strategic Exclusion Priorities

Deprioritize:

- radiology-only multimodal AI
- pathology AI unrelated to peripheral blood/laboratory interpretation
- generic LLM healthcare papers with no laboratory task
- omics-only hematology without morphology or laboratory integration

## Practical Search Interpretation Guidance

### A paper is especially valuable if it includes:

- human hematology context
- peripheral smear images
- structured CBC or analyzer variables
- multimodal fusion
- case-level decision support
- external validation or multi-center testing
- uncertainty, calibration, or explainability

### A paper is strategically useful even if not fully aligned when it contributes:

- fusion methods transferable to hematology
- reasoning architecture suitable for laboratory interpretation
- benchmark design for multimodal clinical AI

## Final Strategic Output Expected

Once the search and screening are complete, the literature should allow you to answer:

1. Is multimodal clinical-laboratory intelligence in hematology a sufficiently distinct niche?
2. Is there enough precedent to make the topic credible?
3. Is there still enough gap to make the topic publishable and doctoral-worthy?
4. Should the thesis center primarily on:
   - multimodal fusion,
   - trustworthy deployment,
   - or a later reasoning-aware layer?
