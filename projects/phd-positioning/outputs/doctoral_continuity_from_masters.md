# Doctoral Continuity From the Master's Dissertation

## Core continuity statement

The doctorate should not be framed as a thematic jump away from the master's dissertation. It should be framed as the **next inferential step** in the same research line.

The master's established competence in:

- hematological morphology as a computational object;
- clinically grounded dataset construction and annotation;
- rigorous comparison of AI models for blood-smear analysis;
- robustness concerns under staining variation and external data;
- translational awareness about real laboratory deployment.

The doctorate therefore emerges naturally as:

`isolated-cell morphology analysis` -> `morphology-aware representation` -> `case-level multimodal hematology inference`

## 1. What the leukocyte-classification work already proved

### 1.1 Methodological foundations were already established

The master's dissertation did more than show that a model can classify leukocytes. It established a methodological base for future doctoral work:

- clear formulation of a clinically meaningful hematology problem;
- structured curation of a local morphology dataset;
- specialist-reviewed annotation logic;
- comparison between modern architectures rather than single-model advocacy;
- treatment of difficult and clinically relevant classes, including immature and atypical cells;
- domain-aware augmentation for staining variation;
- external evaluation and explicit concern with generalization;
- reproducibility and open-science orientation.

This matters because the doctorate no longer needs to begin from a generic “can AI work in hematology?” question. That feasibility has already been explored in a focused and scientifically credible way.

### 1.2 Feasibility of AI-based hematological morphology analysis was already validated

The master's showed that peripheral blood smear morphology can be operationalized as a real AI problem, not just as a conceptual ambition. In practical terms, it demonstrated:

- that hematological morphology can be represented computationally with sufficient signal for learning;
- that clinically meaningful leukocyte classes can be discriminated with modern vision models;
- that morphology pipelines can be evaluated under more realistic conditions than toy benchmarks;
- that variability in staining and acquisition meaningfully affects performance and must be modeled rather than ignored.

This is an important strategic point for the doctorate: the proposal is not speculative. It grows out of a line that has already produced empirical evidence, datasets, models, and methodological lessons.

### 1.3 Technical and scientific infrastructure for multimodal expansion was already created

The master's also created infrastructure that now supports multimodal expansion:

- expertise in peripheral blood smear image handling;
- experience with morphology-focused annotation and class design;
- practical understanding of which morphologies are easy, ambiguous, rare, or clinically critical;
- code, experimental routines, and evaluation logic for hematology AI;
- a scientifically grounded understanding of where image-only analysis starts to become insufficient.

That last point is central. Once cell-level morphology is studied seriously, its limits become visible. Real hematology interpretation depends not only on what a cell looks like, but also on:

- the overall pattern across the smear;
- CBC parameters;
- analyzer flags;
- whether abnormalities co-occur across lineages;
- the broader laboratory context of the case.

The doctoral project therefore builds on the master's not by abandoning morphology, but by **placing morphology inside a larger inference system**.

## 2. Reformulated doctoral narrative

The strongest doctoral narrative is:

> The master's dissertation established a robust foundation in AI-based leukocyte morphology analysis from peripheral blood smears. The doctorate advances this line by moving from isolated cell classification to clinically realistic, case-level multimodal inference, integrating morphology with CBC parameters, analyzer flags, and structured laboratory context to build trustworthy laboratory decision-support systems in hematology.

This narrative is strong because it preserves three forms of continuity:

- **domain continuity:** peripheral blood smear hematology remains the anchor;
- **methodological continuity:** AI for morphology remains central;
- **scientific continuity:** robustness, interpretability, and real-world feasibility remain core concerns.

## 3. Why this transition is scientifically mature

The move from leukocyte classification to multimodal hematology is not an arbitrary broadening of scope. It is the next mature research question after a serious morphology dissertation.

Once image-only morphology has been studied, three natural doctoral questions emerge:

1. How should morphology be aggregated beyond isolated cells?
2. How should morphology be combined with structured laboratory evidence?
3. How can such systems remain robust and interpretable under real laboratory variability?

These are doctoral-level questions because they shift the problem from recognition alone to **integration, inference, reliability, and decision support**.

## 4. Strategic wording to use in applications

The strongest concise formulation is:

> My master's dissertation focused on AI-based analysis of leukocyte morphology in peripheral blood smears, including dataset construction, model comparison, and robustness under staining variability. The doctorate extends this same line toward multimodal clinical-laboratory intelligence in hematology, where morphology remains the anchor but is integrated with CBC parameters, analyzer flags, and structured laboratory data for case-level, interpretable decision support.

## 5. What this continuity prevents

This framing helps prevent four common positioning problems:

- the doctorate from looking like “just another classifier”;
- the transition from looking artificial or trend-driven;
- the proposal from becoming too clinical and weakly computational;
- the scientific identity from fragmenting across unrelated AI topics.

## Bottom line

The master's established the **morphology intelligence layer**.  
The doctorate should establish the **multimodal hematology intelligence layer**.

That is the most coherent and defensible continuity line.
