# Refined Scientific Identity

## Central identity

The most coherent long-term identity is:

**a researcher in multimodal hematology intelligence and trustworthy laboratory AI, with peripheral blood morphology as the anchor and case-level decision support as the translational goal**

This identity is stronger than presenting yourself primarily as:

- a generic medical image AI researcher;
- a generic health AI researcher;
- an LLM-in-healthcare researcher;
- a laboratory informatics professional without a clear computational niche.

## 1. Identity layers

### Layer 1. Multimodal hematology intelligence

This should be the clearest short label for the doctoral positioning.

Why it fits:

- preserves continuity with morphology work;
- signals expansion beyond image-only classification;
- keeps the domain narrow enough to be distinctive;
- matches the literature gap identified in the evidence map.

### Layer 2. Trustworthy laboratory AI

This should be the methodological identity underneath the domain label.

Why it fits:

- connects directly to robustness, generalization, uncertainty, and interpretability;
- reflects your laboratory background and translational realism;
- differentiates you from benchmark-driven morphology papers.

### Layer 3. Computational hematopathology

This is a useful adjacent descriptor, but it should be used carefully.

Why it helps:

- it gives the work scientific seriousness;
- it links morphology to diagnostic interpretation rather than only computer vision;
- it broadens the perceived value of the project.

Why it should be secondary:

- your project is not a full hematopathology program;
- the thesis is better centered on peripheral blood and laboratory workflow than on all hematopathology domains.

### Layer 4. Laboratory decision support

This should define the translational endpoint of the research.

Why it fits:

- it tells a clear story about practical value;
- it keeps the work close to real laboratory operations;
- it supports publication paths beyond pure algorithm papers.

## 2. Best self-description for the PhD phase

The strongest concise description is:

> My research focuses on trustworthy multimodal AI for hematology, integrating peripheral blood smear morphology with structured laboratory data to support robust, interpretable, case-level decision support in clinical laboratory medicine.

## 3. Best long-term niche

The niche with the strongest ownership potential is:

**AI systems that connect microscopic morphology, structured laboratory data, and interpretable decision support for real-world hematology workflows**

This niche is strong because it:

- directly leverages your biomedical and laboratory identity;
- remains methodologically rich for computer science;
- is less saturated than generic radiology AI or generic LLM healthcare work;
- can later expand to broader laboratory medicine without losing continuity.

## 4. Identity statements to emphasize

- morphology remains my scientific anchor;
- my work expands from cell-level analysis to case-level laboratory inference;
- my emphasis is on robust and interpretable AI for real laboratory settings;
- multimodal integration is the main scientific expansion;
- decision support is the translational goal, not generic automation hype.

## 5. Identity statements to avoid

- “I work on AI for everything in healthcare.”
- “My PhD is about agentic AI for medicine.”
- “I am moving from hematology to general foundation models.”
- “My main goal is to build a laboratory chatbot.”
- “The thesis will unify all hematology data sources.”

These formulations weaken continuity and make the positioning sound trend-driven or too broad.

## 6. Recommended labels by context

### For edital and formal proposal language

Use:

- multimodal clinical-laboratory intelligence in hematology;
- trustworthy AI for laboratory decision support;
- robust and interpretable hematology AI.

### For advisor conversations

Use:

- morphology-centered multimodal hematology AI;
- case-level laboratory inference;
- multimodal decision support from smear plus CBC/analyzer data.

### For long-term academic identity

Use:

- trustworthy laboratory AI;
- computational hematology;
- multimodal biomedical intelligence for laboratory medicine.

## Bottom line

Your identity should not be “someone who did leukocyte classification and now wants to try AI trends.”

It should be:

**someone building a coherent research program from morphology-centered hematology AI toward trustworthy multimodal laboratory intelligence**
